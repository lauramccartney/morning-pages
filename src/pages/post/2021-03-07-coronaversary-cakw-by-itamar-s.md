Our corona is a year old this week, it is the first and hopefully last birthday of what crashed into our life and changed it like a new, very demanding member of the household. This plague, a form of grief, had us going through all the known stages, a year in we can now trace how we baked our way through all five of them

like so many others, our oven was (and is) our anchor in a this turbulent sea: we baked our denial, melted our dark and bitter chocolate anger and sweetened it into cake, used said cake as a bargain to be spared and then to comfort us when all the feelings were spent. They say the last stage is acceptance, we get that in glimmers, it comes and goes; we are still waiting on it to settle but we’ll have a slice of cake while we wait, thank you very much: a cake that is pure wholesome tenderness, quivering on the edge of collapse but doesn’t. a cake that gives easy and wants nothing in return, that is simple to make, pure of taste but has a little difference, one that needs no adornment or elaboration to enhance its clean beauty but would be grateful for a pour of cold cream and a scatter of blush fruit, if you are so inclined.

We baked this first around this time last year when we thought it’ll all be over soon and that while we missed mother’s day, we will be with our families for Passover and need a flourless cake – that’ll be denial – and we baked it again to assuage the anger when we realized we won’t: anger at all and specific government bodies, at bats and pangolins, at the satanic landlords of our restaurants, at curfew breakers, at curfew enforcers, at the person standing too close on the queue to the local market, at the person barking at us for standing too close. We baked this cake as a bargaining chip: if we bake for key workers and clap on Thursdays, if we bake to raise money for Beirut, raise funds for captain Tom, if we bake for our neighbors, customers, anyone who needs sweet comfort, than maybe we’ll be spared, maybe it’ll be over soon. And needless to say, we baked it in our sadness, or rather – when we had no feelings left to feel.

So where are we now, after a full four seasons of this? Now we pause, take a deep breath, and bake: not for a virus, it is not our corona that is one year old this week but the new us – for better or worse a new version of us was born a year ago, one that has been through the plague and made it through (knock on wood) and while we are still unsure what the new us will grow into; untrained yet to deal with what is in store for us but also full of possibility for all the incredible things we will do, when we grow up. So we mark this milestone with a little celebration and a very good cake. Maybe this is acceptance.



















***Coronaversarry flourless, dairy free delicious chocolate cake***

A small cake 7inch (18cm) to celebrate a dubious year



60g coconut oil

100g dark 70% chocolate

70g almond butter (or you can use peanut butter or tahini)

3 eggs (at room temperature)

100g caster sugar

2 tbsp cocoa powder

1 tbsp desiccated coconut

2 tbsp ground almonds



for serving (optional)

80g crème fraiche

1 punnt of rasberries



heat your oven to 180 conventional, 160 fan assist.

melt the coconut oil in a small pan and add the chocolate and the nut butter, mix to melt and combineeverything, then set aside.

place the whole eggs and the sugar in a bowl or beaker and using an electric whisk (it would be very hard to achieve the correct amount of fluffing without one) whip until they puff up, lighten in color and become very foamy – aka as ribbon stage, meaning when you remove the whisk it leave a ribbon like mark in the mix that slowly dissolves away. It should take about 5 minutes on a high speed. Fold the chocolate mix by hand into the egg mix, sprinkle the cocoa, coconut and ground almonds over and continue folding till the mix is homogenous, transfer to a lightly buttered small baking tin (ideally a loose bottom one for ease of removing later). Place in the center of the oven for 10 minutes, rotate the cake for even bake and add another 8 minutes to your timer. Remove from the oven and leave the cake to cool in the tin for at least two hours in a cool place, but not in the fridge. Carefully remove and if serving with crème fraiche and raspberries top it just before serving. Serve at room temperature for the best result. If you are keeping it in the fridge make sure to bring it up to room temperature before serving.
